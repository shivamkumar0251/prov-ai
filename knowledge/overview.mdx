The **Knowledge** layer is the brain of Prov AI. It is where you refine the raw database schema into a high-context "Business Semantic Layer" that the AI can truly understand.

---

## Core Pillars of Knowledge

| Pillar | Description | How to Configure |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Semantics** | Natural language descriptions for models and columns. | **Modeling > Metadata Edit** |
| **Relationships** | Logical connections (joins) between tables. | **Modeling > Relationship Add** |
| **Instructions** | Global rules for reasoning and SQL generation. | **Modeling > Custom Instructions** |
| **Calculated Fields**| Predefined metrics and formulas (formulas). | **Modeling > Calculated Field** |

---

## Why Knowledge Matters

Without knowledge, an AI looks at a column named `tx_amt` and guesses. With knowledge, Prov AI knows:
- `tx_amt` means **Transaction Amount**.
- It should be formatted as **Currency**.
- It relates directly to the **Finance** model via `txn_id`.

### The Feedback Loop
As your team uses Prov AI, you can continuously improve the knowledge layer:
1. **Analyze**: Review query history to see where the AI struggled.
2. **Refine**: Add a **Custom Instruction** or a more detailed **Description**.
3. **Deploy**: Update the models to propagate the fix to all users.

---

## Knowledge Workflow

1. **Import Schema**: Start with raw table metadata.
2. **AI Assist**: Use the **Modeling AI Assistant** to generate initial semantics.
3. **Refine**: Manually tweak descriptions and define business-specific instructions.
4. **Iterate**: Use **Thread Tracing** to identify knowledge gaps.

<Tip>
  Start small. Document your most frequently asked tables first, then expand your knowledge layer as your team scales.
</Tip>

**Dashboards** provide a holistic view of your business performance. Instead of viewing isolated charts, dashboards combine multiple generated insights into a unified, interactive layout.

---

## Types of Dashboards

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="AI-Generated" icon="wand-magic-sparkles">
    The AI dynamically creates a dashboard based on a single broad prompt (e.g., *"Create a Sales Dashboard"*).
  </Card>
  <Card title="Manually Created" icon="hammer">
    Users explicitly select and pin specific generated charts to a blank canvas.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Hybrid" icon="diagram-project">
    A mix of auto-generated components fine-tuned by human analysts.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

---

## Key Features

- **Interactive Layout:** Group related charts together.
- **Global Filters:** Apply a filter (e.g., 'Date Range') across all charts simultaneously.
- **Drill-Downs:** Click on any data point to dive into the underlying metrics natively.
- **Real-Time Updates:** Data refreshes automatically based on your connector schedules.

---

## How it Works

Generating an entire dashboard is as easy as asking a single question:

<Steps>
  <Step title="User Prompts the AI">
    > *"Create a Q4 sales dashboard comparing regional performance and top products."*
  </Step>
  <Step title="AI Synthesizes the Request">
    The engine determines the required metrics (Revenue Trend, Top Products, Regional Map).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Auto-Layout">
    The system generates the multiple necessary SQL queries, renders the charts, and intelligently arranges them on a grid layout.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Best Practices & Warnings

<Warning>
**Dashboard Fatigue:**
- **Too many charts reduce clarity:** Keep dashboards focused on a single business theme (e.g., "Marketing KPIs" vs "Support Tickets").
- **Inconsistent metrics confuse users:** Rely on your Semantic Layer to ensure strict consistency across widgets.
</Warning>

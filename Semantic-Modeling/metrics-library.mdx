The **Metrics Library** is a centralized repository where all critical business metrics are defined, vetted, and managed. It acts as the single source of truth for the Prov AI engine.

<Warning>
**The Problem Without a Library:**
If the Marketing team defines "Active Users" as anyone who opened an email, but Product defines it as someone who logged in, reports will never match. Chaos ensues.
</Warning>

---

## Why You Need a Metrics Library

By enforcing metric definitions globally, Prov AI ensures:
1. **Consistency:** No more conflicting reports between teams.
2. **AI Accuracy:** The LLM references the actual formula instead of guessing what "Profit" means.
3. **Trust:** Executives can trust the numbers they see on dashboards.

---

## Example Definitions

Defining a metric involves giving it a clear name, a human-readable description for the AI, and the underlying logic:

```yaml
Metric: Revenue
Description: Total gross revenue from completed orders before refunds.
Formula: SUM(Price × Quantity)
Filters: order_status = 'completed'
```

```yaml
Metric: Net Profit
Description: Bottom line profit after subtracting all associated costs.
Formula: Revenue - Cost_of_Goods_Sold
```

<Note>
Once a metric is registered in the library, any user can type *"Show me Net Profit for Q3"* and the AI will perfectly execute your approved formula.
</Note>

**Relationships** define how separate tables in your database are connected to one another. Properly configuring joins is what allows the Prov AI engine to answer complex, cross-functional business questions seamlessly.

<Info>
If your database already has Foreign Keys strictly defined, Prov AI will attempt to auto-detect these relationships! However, defining them explicitly in the Semantic Layer guarantees flawless join execution.
</Info>

---

## Types of Relationships

Prov AI supports the standard relational logic paradigms used in modern data warehousing:

<Steps>
  <Step title="One-to-One (1:1)">
    A record in Table A relates to exactly one record in Table B.
    *Example:* `Employees` table connected to an `Employee_Details` table.
  </Step>
  <Step title="One-to-Many (1:N)">
    A single record in Table A relates to multiple records in Table B. **(Most Common)**
    *Example:* A single `Customer` can have many `Orders`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Many-to-One (N:1)">
    Multiple records in Table A relate to a single record in Table B.
    *Example:* Multiple `Orders` point to a single `Product`.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Configuring a Relationship

To teach the AI how to traverse your database, you establish the primary and foreign keys:

```text
Table: Customers (Primary)
Column: id

         ↓ (One-to-Many) ↓

Table: Sales (Foreign)
Column: customer_id
```

When a user types: *"Show me the names of customers who bought more than $500 last week"*, the AI knows to `JOIN Sales ON Customers.id = Sales.customer_id` automatically!

**Semantic Modeling** is the critical process of transforming raw, cryptic database table structures into business-friendly, structured definitions. 

This model acts as an intelligent translator between the unrefined data layer and the conversational AI query engine.

---

## Why Semantic Modeling Matters

Databases are built for engineers, not business users. Here is why you must map your semantic layout:

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Without Semantic Modeling" icon="triangle-exclamation">
    - Users struggle with raw names like `tbl_usr_xf_2023`.
    - AI generates hallucinated or incorrect joins.
    - Conflicting formulas for simple concepts like "Revenue".
  </Card>
  <Card title="With Semantic Modeling" icon="check-double">
    - Business terms are standardized across the org.
    - AI understands constraints and relationships instantly.
    - Insights and query generation become 100% reliable.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

---

## Transformational Example

Look at how a Semantic Model fixes raw data for the AI system.

**Raw Database Snapshot:**
```text
Table: tbl_sls_prd_v2
Columns: 
- amt_usd_f (Float)
- org_dst_tx (Varchar)
- dt_ts (Timestamp)
```

**Business-Friendly Semantic Model:**
```text
Table: Sales
Measures:
- Revenue ($)
Dimensions:
- Region
- Purchase Date
```

<Tip>
When a user asks *"Show Revenue by Region"*, Prov AI uses the semantic model to know it should query `tbl_sls_prd_v2` and pull `amt_usd_f` and `org_dst_tx`. This abstraction makes natural language incredibly powerful!
</Tip>

**Schema Mapping** defines exactly how your raw database structures are interpreted by the Prov AI platform.

It is the foundational step that ensures:
1. The AI understands your data correctly.
2. The generated SQL queries are accurate, consistent, and safe.

---

## What are you mapping?

In the Schema Mapping interface, you are aligning physical database objects to logical entities.

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="1. Tables" icon="table">
    Represents logical datasets (e.g., `sales_data_prd_v2` maps to the clear name "Sales").
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="2. Columns & Data Types" icon="columns">
    Every column is interpreted by its type:
    - **String**
    - **Integer / Float**
    - **Date / Timestamp**
    - **Boolean**

    Each column is classified as either a **Dimension** (categorical data to group by) or a **Measure** (numeric data to aggregate).
  </Accordion>
  <Accordion title="3. Relationships" icon="link">
    Defines the JOIN keys between tables:
    - **One-to-Many** (e.g., Customers → Orders)
    - **Many-to-One** (e.g., Orders → Products)
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Visual Example

Consider a raw Database view. Schema mapping translates it cleanly for the AI:

```yaml
Logical Table: "Sales"
  ├── Measures:
  │    └── revenue (Number)
  ├── Dimensions:
  │    ├── region (String)
  │    └── purchase_date (Date)

Logical Relationship:
  Sales.customer_id  ---(Many-to-One)--->  Customers.id
```

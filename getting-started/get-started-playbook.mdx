Welcome! This playbook is designed to guide you through your first week with Prov AI, focusing on the core concept of **Context Engineering**.

## What “Context Engineering” Means in Prov AI

In Prov AI, you shape the AI’s behavior mainly through three layers:

1.  **Semantics (Data Modeling & MDL)**: How tables, columns, joins, and metrics are described so the AI understands your business logic.
2.  **Instructions**: Reusable rules that dictate SQL generation, chart styles, and summaries.
3.  **Question-SQL Pairs**: "Gold-standard" examples that link natural language questions directly to verified SQL.

<Info>
  **Pro Tip**: 
  - **Semantics** = The Dictionary
  - **Instructions** = The House Rules
  - **Question-SQL Pairs** = The Worked Examples
</Info>

Your job as a context engineer is to push as much knowledge as possible into these three layers, so ad-hoc questions become safe, consistent, and predictable.

## Project Scoping & Domain Boundaries

Avoid the “One Giant Graph” trap!

<Tip>
  **Best Practice**: Create separate Prov AI projects per business use case / domain, instead of building one huge, mixed graph for the entire company.
</Tip>

### Why separate projects?
- **Avoid Collisions**: "Revenue" means different things to Finance and Marketing.
- **Reduce Hallucinations**: Smaller, focused projects reduce the chance of the AI picking the wrong metrics.
- **Improved Governance**: Easier to manage specific Instructions and Question-SQL Pairs per domain.

---

## How to Scope Projects

Use separate projects for things like:
- **Marketing & Growth**: Campaigns, Attribution, CAC, ROAS.
- **Product**: Events, Feature Usage, Retention, Funnels.
- **Sales & Revenue**: Pipeline, Bookings, MRR, ARR.
- **Operations / Supply chain**: Lead times, Yield, Utilization.

---

## Rule of Thumb

If you see these signs, your project is likely too large:
- Long lists of tables that no one understands.
- Multiple conflicting metric definitions colliding.
- Instructions that only apply to a specific team but are global.

## First-Time User Checklist

Use this as a mini playbook for your first Prov AI project:

### Day 1–2: Connect & Model
- Connect your main warehouse (Snowflake / BigQuery / etc.).
- Select 5–10 key tables (Orders, Customers, Subscriptions, Events, etc.).
- Use the **Modeling AI Assistant** to generate initial semantics and relationships.
- **Refine descriptions** for:
  - Targets vs actuals.
  - Key metrics and entity IDs.
  - All main date fields.

### Day 3: Configure Instructions
- **Global Instructions**: Set status exclusions, default date field/ranges, and numeric formatting.
- **Question-Matching**: Add logic for specific business terms like “late delivery” or “YoY comparison”.

<Card title="Best Practice: Global Rules" icon="gavel" iconType="light" href="/usage-guides/global-instructions">
  Add instructions as your "Universal Rules".
</Card>

---

### Day 4–5: Seed Question-SQL Pairs
- Identify 5–10 critical metrics (AOV, LTV, MRR, Churn, etc.).
- Import their **"Gold SQL"** from your existing verified reports.
- Map each metric to a straightforward natural-language question.

<Card title="Best Practice: Gold Queries" icon="database" iconType="light" href="/usage-guides/querying/question-sql-pairs">
  Capture gold-standard queries as Question-SQL Pairs.
</Card>

---

### Day 6: Iterate with Real Questions (Human-in-the-Loop)

Now let real users drive context improvement by asking actual questions from stakeholders (via Slack or the Prov AI UI).

| Answer Type | Resolution |
| :--- | :--- |
| **Correct & Useful** | Save as a Question-SQL Pair immediately. |
| **Correct but poor UX** | Add/refine **Instructions** for charts or summaries. |
| **Wrong or Incomplete** | Fix the root cause in **Semantics** or add a specific **Question-SQL Pair**. |

---

## Feedback Loop

Encourage users to give lightweight feedback:
- *"This should exclude X"* → turn that into an Instruction.
- *"This is correct"* → save it to your Knowledge.

Treat Day 6 as the first feedback loop day—Knowledge should grow based on what users actually ask.

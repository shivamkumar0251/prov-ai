This guide introduces sample datasets designed to help you explore the capabilities of **Prov AI** without initially connecting your own data sources. These datasets provide a diverse range of data points that you can use to practice querying, analyzing, and visualizing data within the Prov AI platform.

---

## E-commerce Dataset (Olist)
This dataset is adapted from the Brazilian E-Commerce Public Dataset on Kaggle. It covers the full lifecycle of an online store:
- **Scope**: Customers, orders, shipping, and product reviews.
- **Period**: 2016 to 2018.
- **Key Note**: Product categories are in Portuguese, but an English translation column is provided.

---

## Card Transaction Dataset
<img src="/images/sample-dataset-2.png" alt="Card Dataset" />

A comprehensive financial dataset combining transaction records, customer info, and card data. 
- **Use Case**: Spend pattern analysis, fraud detection, and customer demographics.
- **Period**: 2010s.
- **Exclusion Note**: Includes `train_fraud_labels.csv` to identify fraudulent transactions.

---

## Hotel Rating Dataset
A synthetic dataset designed to emulate a major hotel platform’s booking and review data.
- **Use Case**: Customer satisfaction, booking behavior, and hotel performance.
- **Period**: 2020 to 2025.
- **Core Table**: `reviews.csv` links users and hotels via `user_id` and `hotel_id`.

---

## Human Resource Dataset
<img src="/images/sample-dataset-3.png" alt="HR Dataset" />

A classic relational database perfect for practicing joins and aggregations.
- **Scope**: Demographic and employment details for a large company.
- **Period**: 1950 to 2002.
- **Key Note**: Uses `9999-01-01` to signify current salary or department assignments.

---

## Supply Chain Dataset
Details the end-to-end supply chain for a fashion and beauty startup.
- **Scope**: Manufacturing, suppliers, sales, and shipping.
- **Key Feature**: Includes performance metrics like defect rates and inspection results.

---

## How to use Sample Datasets

When creating a new project, simply select a dataset from the **‘Start with a sample dataset’** section.

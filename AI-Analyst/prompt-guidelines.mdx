Writing effective queries (prompts) drastically improves the accuracy and detail of AI-generated insights. While Prov AI is incredibly smart, guiding it with clear business terminology ensures perfect results every time.

---

## Tips for Better Prompts

<Steps>
  <Step title="Be Specific">
    Avoid vaguely gesturing at data. State exactly what you are looking for.
    - ❌ *"Sales"*  
    - ✅ *"Total sales in Q4 by region"*
  </Step>
  <Step title="Use Business Terms (Your Semantic Layer)">
    Use the exact terminology defined by your data team in the Semantic Layer. 
    - ❌ *"Show amt_usd"*  
    - ✅ *"Show Revenue"*
  </Step>
  <Step title="Add Explicit Filters">
    If you want a specific slice, define it clearly in the prompt:
    - **Time:** *(last month, Q4, Year to Date)*
    - **Category:** *(by region, for product XYZ)*
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Good vs. Bad Prompts

<CardGroup cols={2}>
  <Card title="Excellent Prompts" icon="circle-check" color="green">
    - *"Total revenue last month by region"*
    - *"Top 5 customers by revenue in 2024"*
  </Card>
  <Card title="Poor Prompts" icon="circle-xmark" color="red">
    - *"Show data"*
    - *"Give insights"*
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

---

<Warning>
**Important Notes on Prompting:**
- **Ambiguous prompts lead to guessed results:** If you don't specify a time frame, the AI will default to an all-time aggregate or recent constraints defined in the Semantic Layer.
- **Missing context reduces accuracy:** If you have multiple "revenue" fields, be specific about which one you want to query.
</Warning>

---

## Best Practices

1. **Start simple, then refine:** Start with a broad question (e.g., *"Show total revenue"*), then use follow-up queries to drill down (*"Break that down by month"*).
2. **Use pre-defined prompts:** Utilize prompt templates or AI-assisted suggestions if you are unsure where to start.

<Note>
Remember: **Better prompts = Better insights.**
</Note>
